The Spacecraft

Discover the technology and design behind NASA's historic Voyager probes.

Overview of Voyager Spacecraft

Both Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 were built to explore Jupiter and Saturn, but they far exceeded their original mission goals. With dozens of instruments, they conducted groundbreaking studies of the outer planets and are now venturing into interstellar space, carrying the Golden Record with them.

Key Components

  • High-Gain Antenna (HGA): Sends and receives data over vast distances.
  • Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs): Provide electrical power from the decay of plutonium-238.
  • Science Instruments: Cameras, spectrometers, magnetometers, and more, used to investigate planets, moons, and cosmic phenomena.
  • Golden Record: A message for any spacefaring civilization that might find Voyager in the distant future.

The spacecraft continue to send back valuable scientific data. Their power sources are expected to operate at least into the mid-2020s.

Primary and Extended Missions

The primary mission goals focused on Jupiter and Saturn, leading to major discoveries such as active volcanoes on Jupiter's moon Io and new insights into the structure of Saturn's rings. Voyager 2 continued on to Uranus and Neptune, completing humanity's first (and so far only) visit to those distant ice giants.
